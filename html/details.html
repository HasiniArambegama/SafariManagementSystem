Yala National Park is a huge area of forest
grassland and lagoons bordering the Indian Ocean, in southeast Sri Lanka.
It’s home to wildlife such as,
leopards, elephants and crocodiles,as well as hundreds of bird species.
Address: Katagamuwa, Yala
Area: 978.8 km²
Hours: Sunday-Saturday 6AM-6PM
(Vap Full Moon Poya Day)

Udawalawe is a small town located in the southern part of the Ratnapura District in Sri Lanka.
It is situated in close to the district's boundary with the Hambantota District and Monaragala District.
Udawalawe is an important habitat for Sri Lankan elephants
Udawalawe is also a good birdwatching site.
Province:Sabaragamuwa
Hours:Sunday-Saturday 6AM-6PM
(Vap Full Moon Poya Day)

Wilpattu National Park is a park located on the island of Sri Lanka.
The unique feature of this park is the existence of "Willus" - Natural, sand-rimmed water basins or
essions that fill with rainwater.
Located in the Northwest coast lowland dry zone of Sri Lanka.
Wilpattuwa is also a good birdwatching site.
Address: Katagamuwa, Mannar
Area 1,316.671km2
Hours:Sunday-Saturday 6AM-6PM
(Vap Full Moon Poya Day)




Kumana National Park in Sri Lanka is renowned for its avifauna,
particularly its large flocks of migratory waterfowl and wading birds.
The park is 391 kilometres southeast of Colombo on Sri Lanka's southeastern coast.
Kumana is contiguous with Yala National Park.
Hours:Sunday-Saturday 6AM-6PM
(Vap Full Moon Poya Day)

Minneriya National Park is a national park in
North Central Province of Sri Lanka. The area was designated as a national park on 12 August 1997,
having been originally declared as a wildlife sanctuary in 1938.
Address: Maradankadawala-Habarana
Large numbers of Sri Lankan elephants are attracted to grass fields
Area: 88.89 km² Hours:Sunday-Saturday 6AM-6PM
(Vap Full Moon Poya Day)

Maduru Oya National Park is a national park of Sri Lanka,
established under the Mahaweli development project and
also acts as a catchment of the Maduru Oya Reservoir.
The park was designated on 9 November 1983.
Maduru Oya is well known for its elephant habitat.
Other than elephants, leopards, sloth bears, Sambhur, spotted and barking deer,
wild boar and wild buffalo are also found there
Address: Manampitiya-Aralaganwila-Maduru Oya Rd
Area: 588.5 km²
Hours:Sunday-Saturday 6AM-6PM
(Vap Full Moon Poya Day)

Lunugamvehera National Park in Sri Lanka was declared in 1995,
with the intention of protecting the catchment area of the Lunugamvehera reservoir and wildlife of the area.
The national park is an important habitat for water birds and elephants.
Area: 235 km²
Address: Uva province and Southern province.
park includes 21 fish species, 12 amphibians, 33 reptiles, 183 birds and 43 mammals.
Sri Lankan elephant, water buffalo, Sri Lankan sambar deer, wild boar, Sri Lankan spotted chevrotain,
grizzled giant squirrel and Sri Lankan axis deer. Hours:Sunday-Saturday 6AM-6PM
(Vap Full Moon Poya Day)
